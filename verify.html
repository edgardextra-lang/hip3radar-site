Common questions

If your question isn't here, ask at hello@hip3radar.xyz.

Why $299/mo — what am I actually paying for?
You're paying for an independent attestation and the right to display the Verified badge on your app. The actual monitoring of your markets is free — we do it for everybody. What you get uniquely is: signed audit trail, public verification URL, embeddable badge SVG, private dashboard with custom thresholds, 24-hour support response, and monthly audit PDF.
Can I pay to pass?
No. The verification threshold is identical for every applicant. If your markets don't meet the bar at observation time, we'll tell you exactly what to fix and you can reapply after 14 days with no additional fee. Our entire business model depends on verification being unbuyable — once you can buy a pass, the badge is worthless to everyone.
What gets reviewed during the 7-day observation?
Six signals per market: oracle drift vs CEX consensus, mark-oracle spread behavior, OI velocity and cap proximity, thin-float exposure (OI / daily volume), funding extremity and persistence, and incident history. You see the same dashboard live — nothing happens in a black box.
What happens if a score drops below threshold after verification?
You get a 72-hour heads-up before the badge state changes publicly. During that window your team can respond, adjust parameters, or provide context. If the threshold is still breached after 72h, the badge turns to "Under Review" for up to 7 days. If the issue is resolved, Verified resumes. If not, verification is paused until you reapply.
Is this endorsed by Hyperliquid Labs?
No. HIP3Radar is independent, third-party infrastructure. We have no affiliation with Hyperliquid Labs or any deployer. That independence is the whole point — if we were affiliated with any stakeholder, the score would be suspect.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes. Monthly subscription, cancel anytime. Verification stays public until the end of your current billing period, then the badge expires. Your public market page remains visible forever (the score is free for everyone, always).
