The signals that precede slashing

Validators slash deployers when oracles are manipulated, markets become illiquid, or OI caps are breached. We catch every precursor, scored 0–100, refreshed every 60 seconds — so you act before they vote.

Oracle drift vs CEX consensus

HL oracle vs Binance + OKX + Bybit median. Validators slash deployers whose oracles diverge >50% from start-of-day price — we alert at >5%, hours before the review window.

Mark-oracle spread attack detection

The JELLY signature: HL mark wicks while oracle stays. If your market shows a coordinated spread widening, you get a DM in 60 seconds.

OI velocity + cap proximity

Track OI growth vs your configured caps. Alerts at 80% utilization, 90%, breach. No more 3am surprises during Asia hours.

Thin-float exposure score

OI / daily volume ratio per market. If your market shape starts looking like JELLY (OI ≫ turnover), you're notified before it's exploitable.

Funding extremity + persistence

Extreme funding signals crowded positioning — the condition that precedes squeeze attacks. Track percentile across your entire market set.

Emergency runbooks

When an alert fires, the dashboard surfaces the validated response: pause market, roll oracle to fallback, escalate to Hypercore. Not guesswork — playbook.

Why this exists

Built after JELLY. Still necessary today.

In March 2025 a single trader used a thin-float meme perp to push $12M in losses onto Hyperliquid's HLP vault, triggering an emergency manual delisting. Every signal this scoreboard tracks would have fired hours before the wipe. But nothing was watching — and nobody independent was publishing what would have been obvious.

An attacker opened a $4.1M short on ~40% of a token's supply, then pumped the spot price 400% on CEXes. The HL oracle followed. The liquidator vault inherited the underwater short at a manipulated price.
— The JELLY incident, post-mortem

HIP-3 unlocked permissionless perp deployment. Every new market is another surface for the same playbook — and now each market has a deployer whose $20M stake is on the line. The scoreboard is for the traders who get hurt first and the deployers who pay second. Neutral, public, free.
